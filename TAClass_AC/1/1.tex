Let $a$ and $b$ be two real numbers. If $a \leq b + \epsilon$ for any $\epsilon > 0$, then $a \leq b$.

\begin{tcolorbox}
	\begin{solution}
		If not, $a>b \implies a-b > 0$. For $\epsilon = \dfrac{a-b}{2} > 0$
		
		$$a \leq b + \dfrac{a - b}{2} = \dfrac{a + b}{2}$$
		
		$\implies a \leq b (\rightarrow \leftarrow)$
	\end{solution}
\end{tcolorbox}
